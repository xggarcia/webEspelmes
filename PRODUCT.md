# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Conscious shoppers who seek handmade, natural, and local alternatives to mass-market goods. They buy intentionally: they read ingredient lists, notice when something is made in Barcelona versus drop-shipped from a warehouse, and value the maker story as part of the product itself. Secondary: gift buyers looking for something with genuine craft and character behind it. They browse on mobile at home, in natural light, often discovering the shop through a recommendation or social post.

## Product Purpose

Espelmes is a bilingual (Catalan / Spanish) online shop for artisan candles made by hand in Barcelona. It offers a catalog, a real-time product configurator (scent, color, container), cart, and Stripe checkout. The admin panel lets the shop owner manage products, orders, customers, inventory, and pricing. Success looks like a customer completing a purchase feeling like they bought something with a real person behind it, not a product.

## Brand Personality

Warm, honest, crafted. The brand speaks plainly, with warmth rather than polish. It doesn't perform artisanship; it embodies it. Voice is direct and sensory. Tone in Catalan first, Spanish second; both should feel natural, not translated.

## Anti-references

- **Generic Shopify templates**: grid-hero-product-section patterns, identical card layouts, "SALE" banners, default Shopify fonts. No template energy.
- **Luxury minimalism**: cold white space, hairline type, untouchable brand distance. Diptyque / Jo Malone aesthetic: aspirational to the point of alienating.
- **Cottagecore overload**: forced rusticity, distressed textures, "hand-lettered" fonts, Mason jar photography. Nostalgic but not premium, and not honest about what this actually is.

## Design Principles

1. **Made by hands, not by committee.** Every screen should feel like a person considered it, not a template. Asymmetry, imperfect rhythm, and specific choices signal craft.
2. **Honest warmth.** Warmth comes from real material decisions: cream linen tones, grain, serif type. Not from stock photography of cozy blankets or overused "hygge" signifiers.
3. **Sensory clarity.** The product is the hero. Photography, the configurator, and scent descriptions get the space. UI recedes; product comes forward.
4. **Rooted specificity.** Barcelona, Catalan first. The maker story is not decoration; it is the differentiator. Show it plainly and let it carry weight.
5. **Earned restraint.** Quiet space signals care. But restraint is earned by what's present, not by removing everything. A page should feel considered, not empty.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA baseline. Keyboard navigable. Screen reader support for product configurator (color and scent selections must have accessible labels). Reduced-motion media query respected for all animations.
